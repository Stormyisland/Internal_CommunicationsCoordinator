
An Internal Communications Coordinator is the strategic link between an organization's leadership and its employees, ensuring information flows effectively to build a cohesive, informed, and engaged workforce . This role is central to shaping company culture and supporting business objectives .

🔑 Key Responsibilities
The core of the role revolves around creating and distributing content to keep employees informed and connected.

Content Creation & Editing: You will write, edit, and publish clear, engaging content for various internal channels. This includes daily news stories, leadership messages, newsletters, intranet articles, and employee spotlights .

Channel Management: You will manage and maintain the company's internal communication tools, such as the intranet and Microsoft Viva Engage. This involves curating content, managing site architecture, and ensuring the platforms are effective and up-to-date .

Event Support: You will support the planning and logistics of key internal events like town halls, company updates, and webinars . This can include content development, logistical coordination, and pre- and post-event communications .

Measurement & Reporting: You will monitor engagement metrics (e.g., newsletter opens, intranet traffic) and report on the effectiveness of communications to stakeholders, helping to refine future strategies .

Project Coordination: You will coordinate projects with various stakeholders, including graphic designers, HR, marketing, and subject matter experts, to ensure timely delivery of communications .

🛠️ Required Skills & Experience
To succeed in this role, you typically need a blend of strong communication skills and relevant experience.

Education & Experience: A post-secondary degree in Communications, Public Relations, Journalism, Marketing, or a related field is often required . Employers generally seek 1-4 years of experience in a communications or marketing role, with more senior positions requiring 5-8 years .

Core Skills:

Exceptional Writing & Editing: You must have outstanding written, oral, and presentation skills, with a keen attention to detail and the ability to adapt tone for different internal audiences .

Technical Proficiency: Familiarity with key platforms is highly valued, including:

Microsoft 365 Suite: Deep knowledge of SharePoint, Teams, Outlook, Word, and PowerPoint is often essential .

Content Management Systems: Experience with web publishing tools like WordPress is an asset .

Other Tools: Experience with Canva, email platforms (e.g., Poppulo), and survey tools (e.g., Qualtrics) is a plus .

Project Management: You need strong organizational skills to manage multiple projects, prioritize tasks, and meet deadlines .

💰 Salary & Career Outlook
The salary for this role in Canada varies based on experience, location, and company size.

Base Salary: Glassdoor reports an average base pay of approximately $53,000 CAD** per year, with the typical range falling between **$45,000 and $61,000 CAD .

Specific Ranges: Some companies, like Aviso, list a range of $55,000 - $65,000 CAD . The average hourly pay in Vancouver was reported at approximately $31/hr as of mid-2026 .

Career Progression: Entry-level coordinators can progress to specialist or senior specialist roles, and eventually to managerial positions. The seniority level for the role can range from early-career to mid-senior . A mid-level role at ISL (5-8 years experience), for example, offers a higher range of $70,000 - $80,000 CAD .

🎤 How to Prepare for an Interview
Interviews for this role often focus on behavioral questions to assess your past performance and problem-solving skills .

Common Questions: Expect to be asked about times you've handled a complex or sensitive communication, managed conflicting priorities, tailored messages for different audiences, or measured the success of a campaign .

Key Topics: Be ready to discuss your experience with content management systems, intranet management, and how you collaborate with cross-functional teams . You may also be asked to explain how you would propose a solution for a real-life internal communications case .
